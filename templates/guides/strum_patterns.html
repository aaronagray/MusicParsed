Strum patterns

Song chords indicate when to transition between chords, but usually donâ€™t specify how to play the chords on the right hand. I recommend using a strum pattern.

A strum pattern is a regularly repeating sequence that you play independently of the singing. The most important step is developing a sense of rhythm. Learn how to count, and start simple.

Suppose that the chords transition every 4 beats. The simplest strum pattern you can play is to strum down (D) once for every new chord. Other simple strum patterns are strumming every 2 beats and strumming every beat.

  1. D D
  2. D D D D
  3. D D D D D D D D

.

You can also learn how to strum up (U). I strum down with the nail on my index finger and up with the fleshy part of the finger.

  1. DUDUDUDUDUDUDUDU
  2. D DUD DUD DUD DU
  3. D DU UDUD DU UDU
    • This is the island strum and takes a while to master.
